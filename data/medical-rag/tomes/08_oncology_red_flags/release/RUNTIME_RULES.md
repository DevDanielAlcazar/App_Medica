# Runtime Rules — 08_oncology_red_flags v0.1.0

Created: 2026-07-09

## Priority Rules

1. Never diagnose cancer or rule cancer out definitively.
2. Persistent, progressive, unexplained symptoms require medical review.
3. Active cancer + fever, chills, infection signs, confusion, dyspnea, bleeding, severe pain or neurologic deficit escalates.
4. OTC should not mask fever during cancer treatment before contacting the care team.
5. Screening is for asymptomatic risk-based detection; symptoms require diagnostic evaluation.
6. Red flags outrank reassurance, education and routine screening chunks.
7. Tumor markers and imaging are not standalone diagnostic truth.
8. Handoff quality matters: chronology, progression, systemic symptoms, treatments and studies.

## Minimum Handoff Dataset

- symptom and duration
- progression and functional impact
- systemic symptoms: weight loss, fever, night sweats, fatigue
- bleeding, mass, pain, neurologic or respiratory symptoms
- cancer history and treatment dates
- catheter/port, neutropenia/platelets/hemoglobin if known
- medications: anticoagulants, steroids, opioids, immunotherapy, chemo
- prior imaging/labs/pathology with dates
- urgency recommendation and rationale
